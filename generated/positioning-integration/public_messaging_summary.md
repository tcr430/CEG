## Old positioning summary
- Public copy leaned on broad `AI outbound copilot` language.
- The homepage presented SDR teams, SaaS founders, and agencies as roughly equal top-level audiences.
- The public message focused more on generation capability and outbound quality than on workflow ownership.
- Pricing copy emphasized broad outbound motions and operational headroom, but it did not clearly anchor the product in an agency-first workflow model.
- The sign-in area was functional, but it did not reinforce the workflow-system framing.

## New positioning summary
- The homepage now presents the product as `the operating system for agency-grade hyperpersonalized cold email`.
- The primary fit is now explicit: small-to-mid outbound agencies serving B2B clients with manual-heavy personalization workflows.
- Public copy now emphasizes workflow ownership over one-off generation, including sender and campaign context, prospect research, sequence workflow, reply handling, operational memory, and campaign learning.
- The trust model is now visible in public copy through human-review language: AI proposes actions inside a reviewable workflow rather than automating outbound.
- Pricing messaging now frames plans around workflow depth and operating headroom for agency-style work, while keeping the existing live plan names intact.

## Copy principles used
- Prefer `workflow system`, `operating system`, `controlled outbound execution`, `operational memory`, and `campaign learning` over generic copilot language.
- Keep claims grounded in actual repo capabilities: sender-aware context, prospect research, sequences, reply intelligence, quality checks, and stored history.
- Avoid claims about autonomous outbound, auto-sending, conversion lift, or reply-rate lift.
- Keep secondary supported audiences truthful, but clearly subordinate to the primary agency ICP.
- Preserve a conservative, credible tone instead of using hype or inflated performance language.

## Any places left intentionally unchanged
- Public plan names remain `Free`, `Pro`, and `Agency` in pricing surfaces because this task focused on messaging, not billing/package renaming. Internal plan codes and checkout flows were left untouched for compatibility.
- The product name `Outbound Copilot` was left in place because the task was a positioning rewrite, not a rename pass.
- Authenticated in-app upgrade prompts, settings copy, onboarding copy, and deeper product workflow surfaces were left unchanged because this pass was scoped to public-facing messaging.
- Sign-in metadata remained simple and functional rather than becoming a larger marketing surface.
